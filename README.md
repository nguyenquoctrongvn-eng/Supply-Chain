# Supply Chain Analysis

## Overview

This project analyzes a global supply chain dataset using SQL Server and Power BI to uncover business insights related to sales performance, customer behavior, product profitability, and logistics efficiency. The dataset contains **65,536 rows and 53 columns**, providing detailed transactional and operational data across multiple business areas.

The dataset was obtained from Kaggle.

The products in the dataset belong to multiple categories such as:

* Clothing
* Sports
* Electronic Supplies

The SQL analysis includes:

* JOIN operations
* Common Table Expressions (CTEs)
* Window Functions
* Aggregations
* Ranking techniques
* KPI calculations

Key business metrics analyzed:

* Total Revenue
* Total Profit
* Customer Segmentation
* Product Performance
* Late Delivery Rate
* Discount Impact
* Geographic Sales Distribution

The processed data was connected to Power BI to build an interactive business intelligence dashboard featuring:

* Sales and profit overview
* Revenue trends over time
* Top-performing products
* Customer analysis
* Shipping and delivery insights
* Profitability analysis


## Key Insights

### Product Performance

* Revenue is highly concentrated in a small number of products, with the top 10 products contributing more than **86% of total revenue**.
* Heavy discounting significantly reduces profitability, with several high-discount products generating negative profit.

### Customer Analysis

* Consumer and Corporate segments are the primary revenue drivers, contributing nearly **90% of total revenue**.
* Profit margins remain relatively consistent across customer segments, averaging around **12%**.

### Supply Chain Performance

* Late delivery remains a major supply chain issue, indicating inefficiencies in order fulfillment and logistics operations.
* Delivery performance varies significantly across shipping modes, suggesting operational differences in shipping efficiency.
